The M829 is an armor-piercing fin stabilized discarding sabot shot (APFSDS) it was modelled at the ballistics research laboratory at Aberdeen proving grounds. The round was specifically designed for the 120mm main gun on the Abrams M1A1 and M1A2 main battle tanks.
The shell has a total weight of 18.6kg and can reach a velocity of 1670 meter per second and can penetrate up to 540mm of rolled homogeneous armour. The original M829 is no longer in production and has been succeeded by the M829A1.
The A1 variant saw combat during Operation Desert Storm, earning the nickname "silver bullet" by american tank crews due to its effectiveness agains Iraqi T-55s and T-72M tanks

The 3BM42 is a Soviet armor-piercing fin stabilized discarding sabot shot. The projectile is a double tungsten alloy covered with steel intended to penetrate non reactive armor, such as Chobham armor.
The 3BM42 round was designed to be used by the 125mm smoothbore gun mounted on many Soviet and Russian main battle tanks, including the T-72, T-80, T-90 and many more. There are many variations and designations of the 3BM round but the 3BM42 was given the name "Mango" by the researchers developing the round. The round weigh approximately 20.4kg and has a muzzle velocity of 1700 meters per second.
